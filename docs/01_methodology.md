# Methodology

## Governing Question

The governing question is not whether a process exists, a policy is mapped, or a log was produced.

The governing question is:

> Can this movement bind consequence right now?

## Consequence-Bearing Movement

A movement is consequence-bearing when it can create, modify, release, deny, escalate, expose, deploy, authorize, revoke, transfer, or materially affect an operational standing.

Examples:

- AI recommendation
- operator approval
- payment release
- customer escalation
- data access
- model update
- clinical decision support
- workflow override
- vendor handoff
- policy exception
- system deployment

## Admission Dimensions

A movement is not admitted by intention. It is admitted only when required dimensions hold:

| Dimension | Admission Question |
|---|---|
| Authority | Who or what has standing to admit the movement? |
| Evidence | What proof supports the movement before binding? |
| Custody | Has evidence chain and operational responsibility remained intact? |
| Standing | Is the admitting authority still active and within scope? |
| Policy | Does the movement satisfy governing rules and exclusions? |
| Refusal | What condition blocks the movement if missing or invalid? |
| Revalidation | What future change forces re-check before continuation? |
| Receipt | What durable proof records the admission/refusal decision? |
| Replay | Can a later reviewer reproduce why movement was admitted or refused? |

## Verdicts

| Verdict | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ADMIT | consequence may bind because required dimensions hold |
| HOLD | consequence may not bind yet because required evidence/standing is incomplete |
| REFUSE | consequence must not bind because a blocking condition is active |
| NO_PROVABLE_ADMISSION | consequence path appears capable of becoming real without sufficient proof |

## Black Path Definition

A black path is a consequence-bearing path where operational effect may occur without lawful, evidential, structural, and replayable admission.

Black paths are the highest-value diagnostic findings because they expose action becoming reality outside provable control.
