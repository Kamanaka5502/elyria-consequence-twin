# Elyria Consequence Twin — Buyer One-Page

## One-Line Position

```text
A live consequence-admission twin that shows whether action can bind before execution becomes operationally real.
```

## The Problem

Organizations increasingly rely on AI agents, workflow automation, access systems, payment flows, approval chains, model updates, vendor handoffs, and customer-operation tooling.

Most systems can answer:

```text
what happened
who approved it
where the control is documented
which workflow step ran
```

They often cannot answer, before the action binds:

```text
Did valid authority exist?
Was standing active?
Was required evidence present?
Was custody preserved?
Was refusal logic checked?
Was a receipt created?
Can the verdict be replayed later?
Where did consequence become real without proof?
```

## The Elyria Answer

Elyria Consequence Twin classifies consequence-bearing movement before execution binds.

It produces:

```text
ADMIT — consequence may bind
HOLD — proof is incomplete
REFUSE — movement is blocked
NO_PROVABLE_ADMISSION — black-path consequence risk
```

## What Makes It Different

This is not a generic GRC dashboard, process map, org chart, or incident log.

Elyria models:

```text
action attempted
path of consequence
authority condition
standing condition
evidence references
custody condition
refusal condition
receipt basis
replay basis
black-path exposure
```

The system does not just show what exists. It shows what can become operationally real and what must not bind.

## Executive Output

The flagship output is a Consequence Exposure Graph.

```text
green  — admitted consequence path
red    — refused movement
black  — unprovable consequence path
```

Black paths are the executive review surface: they expose movement that is attempting to become real without sufficient durable proof.
