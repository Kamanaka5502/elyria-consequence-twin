# Client Intake Questions

## Organization Context

1. What business unit, product, or operational area should the scan cover?
2. Which action types create the most concern: AI, workflow, access, approval, payment, deployment, customer operations, vendor handoff, or policy exception?
3. What consequence can occur if the action is wrong, unauthorized, unsupported, or unreplayable?

## Movement Identification

4. What actions can create, release, deny, escalate, expose, deploy, revoke, or transfer operational standing?
5. Which systems or people can trigger those actions?
6. Which actions are automated, AI-assisted, or workflow-driven?
7. Which actions require approval before effect?
8. Which actions can bypass approval under exception or override?

## Authority

9. Who or what has authority to admit the action?
10. Where is that authority documented?
11. Can authority expire, change, or be revoked?
12. How is authority scope checked at the time of action?

## Evidence

13. What evidence must exist before action binds?
14. Where is the evidence stored?
15. Is evidence available before the action or only after the fact?
16. Can a later reviewer reproduce the decision from the evidence?

## Custody

17. Who owns the evidence chain?
18. Where can evidence be modified, lost, overridden, or detached from the action?
19. Which systems preserve custody across handoffs?

## Refusal and Hold

20. What conditions must block the action?
21. What conditions should hold the action pending more evidence?
22. Are refusal conditions enforced by system, policy, or human judgment?
23. What happens when required evidence is missing?

## Revalidation

24. What changes should force revalidation?
25. Does movement remain valid if policy, role, risk, model version, vendor status, customer status, or authorization changes?

## Receipts and Replay

26. What receipt is produced when action is admitted, held, or refused?
27. Can the receipt prove authority, evidence, custody, and standing at bind time?
28. Can the decision be replayed later without relying on memory or informal explanation?
